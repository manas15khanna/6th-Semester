![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_bb7bb35a.png)

_Fig. 1: Creating Project_

  
  

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_53081cb1.png)

_Fig. 1.1: my_scrum_project_

  

**Step 2: Understanding the Interface Factors**

1. **Summary:** Summary tab is your “Mission Control”. It provides a high-level overview of the project’s health, including recent activity, upcoming deadlines and statistics on “Done” vs “To-Do” issues.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_506cc7b4.png)

_Fig. 2: Summary Tab_

  

2. **List:** It is a spread-sheet like interface. It allows you to quickly add, edit, and filter issues (User Stories, Tasks, Bugs) without opening individual pages, Its best for bulk editing.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_5f6609da.png)

_Fig. 2.1: List View_

  

3. **Board:** It is the visual heart of scrum. It shows the “Active Sprint”. Usually, it is divided into vertical columns: To Do, In Progress, and Done. You move “cards” (tasks) across these columns as work progresses.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_6301dd80.png)

_Fig. 2.2: Board View_

  

4. **Backlog:** It is the “waiting room” for all work. Here, you create User Stories that haven’t been assigned to a sprint yet. You also use this tab to plan your Sprints by dragging items from the Backlog into a Sprint container.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_2715c4c4.png)

_Fig. 2.3: Backlog View_

  

5. **Timeline (formally Roadmap):** Timeline is a Gantt-Chart style view. It allows “Epics” (large bodies of work) wrapped against a calendar. It helps stakeholders see how long a project will take and how different tasks overlap.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_e0aeef52.png)

_Fig. 2.4: Timeline View_

  

6. **Forms:** This feature allows you to create internal forms to collect information. For example, if a team-mate wants to request a new feature, they fill out the form and Jira automatically creates a task in your backlog.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_18c76ae0.png)

_Fig. 2.5: Forms View_

  

7. **Calendar:** This view maps your issues to specific dates based on their “Due Date”. It provides a traditional monthly/weekly view of what needs to be finished and when.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_75bf8c32.png)

_Fig. 2.6: Calendar View_

**Experiment 7**

**Creating Sprint Reports (Burndown Chart)**

  

**Objective**

To generate and analyze Sprint Report and Burndown Chart in Jira.

  

**Theory**

- A Burndown Chart is a visual measurement tool that shows how much work is remaining in a Sprint versus the time available to complete it.The goal is to "burn down" the total effort (measured in Story Points or Hours) to zero by the end of the Sprint.
    
- A typical chart has two main lines:
    

1. **The Ideal Burndown Line (The "Straight" Line):** This is a theoretical straight line from the top left to the bottom right. It shows exactly how much work you _should_ be completing every day to finish on time.
    
2. **The Actual Burndown Line (The "Jagged" Line):** This shows the real-time progress of your team. Every time a User Story or Task is moved to "Done," the line drops.
    

  

**Procedure**

**Step 1: Navigate to Reports Section**

1. After completing a sprint, navigate to the Dashboard and select the chosen project “my_scrum_project”
    
2. Click Reports.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_f64a2bdd.png)

_Fig.1: Reports View_

**Step 2: Open the Sprint Report**

1. After completing a sprint, navigate to the Dashboard and select the chosen project “my_scrum_project”
    
2. Click Reports and select Burndown Chart.
    

![](file:///tmp/lu108776ce5jg.tmp/lu108776ce5r7_tmp_bba40819.png)

_Fig.2: Burndown Chart_

  

**Step 3: Analyze Burndown Chart**

1. Burndown chart shows the total work remaining for the current sprint, and the likelihood of your team completing the sprint on time. It also shows a summary of scope changes and work done throughout the sprint.
    
2. The red line shows how much work remains in the sprint.
    
3. The grey line shows the ideal progress rate

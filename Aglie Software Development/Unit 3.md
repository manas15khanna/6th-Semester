# Matrices and Measurements 
- Quantitative Indicators 
- Performance, progress, quality, time, cost, human resource 
## Types of Agile Matrices 
1. Delivery or process (Improve planning and flow)
	- Velocity 
	- Cycle Time 
	- Lead Time
	- Sprint burndown
	- Throughput (number of items completed in a period)
2. Quality (Build reliable software)
	- Defect Density 
	- Test Coverage
	- Rework Rate 
3. Product or business (Validate the Business Impact)
	- Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)
	- Retention Rate  
	- Cost Savings/Revenue
4. Team Help (Maintain Long Term productivity)
	- Team Happiness Index
	- Team capacity 
	- Team Stability 
## Leading Matrices 
Predicts the future
- Future cycle time  
- Future test coverage 
## Lagging Matrices
Shows the past result 
- Revenue tracked 
- Customer satisfaction 
# Managing Business 
Ensuring that Business Stakeholders are actively engaged, informed or aligned with the product/project throughout its lifecycle. 
In Agile, Business participation is continuous 
## Involvement of Business 
- Prevents misunderstandings
- Enables Faster decision making 
- Aligns the priority 
- Ensure Products for real business problems 
- Share product vision
